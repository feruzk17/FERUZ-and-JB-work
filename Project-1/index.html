The history of artificial intelligence (AI) began in the 1950s, with pioneering work by Alan Turing and John McCarthy laying the foundational groundwork12. In 1950, Turing proposed his famous Turing Test to evaluate machine intelligence, while McCarthy coined the term "artificial intelligence" in 1955 and organized the pivotal Dartmouth Workshop in 1956, which is considered the formal birth of AI as an academic discipline
